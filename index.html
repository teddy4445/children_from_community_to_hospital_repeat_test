Community to hospital predictor

A Data-driven Guide for Repeated Testing in Pediatric Emergency Departments of Laboratory Testing Previously Performed in the Community

About

About The Project

Blood tests performed on children in community settings are sometimes repeated when they visit pediatric emergency departments. However, when the time between tests is short, some of these repeated tests appear unnecessary, leading to increased inconvenience for children, parental anxiety, added strain on the healthcare system, and delays in clinical decision-making. To minimize such negative phenomenon, this study aimed to analyze the circumstances under which blood tests conducted in the community can be used rather than repeated using a data-driven-based modeling.

Laboratory tests for complete blood count, electrolytes, and C-reactive protein, conducted in the community for children aged three months to 18 years, were analyzed and compared with repeat tests performed in hospitals within 12 hours. The results of these repeated hospital tests were evaluated against the initial community tests. A repeat test was considered justified if there was an absolute change of 20% or more. Based on this definition, we developed an optimal decision tree model serving as an easy-to-use guideline for clinicians.

Using the proposed data-driven guidelines, clinicians can reduce the amount of unnecessary repeated laboratory tests without compromising clinical performance.

  • Real time: online solution under a second.
  • Explanabile: provides an simple explanation to each decision.
  • Minimal data: do not required long time data gathering or expensive clinical tests.
